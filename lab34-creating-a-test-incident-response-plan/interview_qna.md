# 🧠 Interview Q&A — Lab 34: Creating a Test Incident Response Plan (IRP)

---

## 1) What is an Incident Response Plan (IRP)?
An IRP is a documented process that explains how an organization detects, responds to, contains, eradicates, and recovers from cybersecurity incidents, including roles, escalation, and communication.

---

## 2) Why is an IRP important during real incidents?
Because incidents are stressful and time-sensitive. An IRP reduces confusion, improves coordination, speeds containment, and ensures proper documentation and evidence handling.

---

## 3) What frameworks can guide IRP creation?
Common frameworks include:
- **NIST** (Incident Response Lifecycle)
- **SANS** (Incident Handling process)
They provide structured stages and best practices for response.

---

## 4) What roles were defined in this lab’s IRP?
- Incident Response Manager (IRM)
- HOA Security Lead
- IT Support

---

## 5) What is the Incident Response Manager responsible for?
The IRM coordinates the entire response:
- classifies severity
- assigns tasks
- approves containment actions
- manages timelines
- ensures reporting and communications

---

## 6) What is the HOA Security Lead responsible for?
The Security Lead focuses on investigation and scope:
- validates alerts and IOCs
- assesses impact
- recommends containment/remediation
- ensures evidence handling and logging

---

## 7) What is IT Support responsible for?
IT Support executes technical actions:
- account resets / MFA enforcement
- isolating systems
- restoring services
- patching and recovery assistance

---

## 8) Why define severity levels (Low/Medium/High)?
Severity levels standardize decision-making so the response matches the impact. They clarify:
- who must be notified
- response time targets
- whether executives/legal/PR must be engaged

---

## 9) What is an example of a Low severity incident?
A minor security event with minimal impact and no confirmed data exposure, such as a single blocked phishing email or a contained minor misconfiguration.

---

## 10) What is an example of a High severity incident?
A confirmed breach or major disruption such as:
- ransomware
- sensitive data exposure
- large-scale outage
- confirmed attacker persistence

---

## 11) Why is a communication plan included in an IRP?
Because communication failures cause delays and mistakes. The IRP ensures:
- proper internal coordination
- controlled external communications (if needed)
- minimal leakage of sensitive details

---

## 12) Why is evidence handling important in incident response?
Evidence supports investigations and accountability. Poor handling can:
- destroy forensic data
- prevent root cause identification
- weaken legal/compliance outcomes

---

## 13) What is a tabletop exercise?
A tabletop exercise is a discussion-based simulation where the team walks through the IRP using a scenario, role-plays decisions, and identifies gaps without touching production systems.

---

## 14) What scenario was used in this tabletop exercise?
A phishing attack leading to credential capture and suspicious login activity, suggesting potential unauthorized access.

---

## 15) What was the main takeaway from this lab?
A clear, documented IRP with defined roles, escalation steps, and a validated tabletop walkthrough improves readiness and helps the team respond faster and more consistently.
